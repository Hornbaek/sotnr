# Shadows of the Nine Realms — Core Mechanics

## The Round Structure

Shadows of the Nine Realms plays out in rounds, each consisting of two phases:

### 1. HERO PHASE
Players take turns in any order they choose. On your turn, play two ability cards — one for its **TOP action** and one for its **BOTTOM action**. You may perform these actions in either order.

### 2. MONSTER PHASE
Each monster type activates according to its AI card, performing actions based on its programmed behavior and preferred targets.

---

## Ability Cards

Each hero has unique ability cards. Each card has:

- **TOP ACTION:** Typically an attack or aggressive maneuver
- **BOTTOM ACTION:** Typically movement, defense, or utility
- **INITIATIVE NUMBER:** Determines turn order within the Hero Phase (lower goes first)

After playing cards, they are placed in your discard pile. When you would draw cards with none left, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck. If you need to shuffle but have cards in hand, suffer 1 Exhaustion instead.

---

## Movement & Positioning

The hex grid map uses standard hex movement. Each movement point allows you to move to an adjacent hex.

| Situation | Effect |
|---|---|
| Normal Terrain | 1 movement point per hex |
| Difficult Terrain | 2 movement points per hex |
| Flanking (behind target) | +2 to attack roll |
| Higher Ground | +1 to attack roll |
| Obstacle | Blocks line of sight and movement |

---

## Exhaustion

Exhaustion represents your hero's fatigue and dwindling resources. When you suffer Exhaustion, place an Exhaustion Token on your character board. If you ever have Exhaustion Tokens equal to your maximum health, you are **Exhausted** and cannot act until you Rest.
