# Shadows of the Nine Realms — Campaign System

The campaign spans multiple scenarios with persistent progression, story consequences, and evolving challenges. Everything is tracked physically through Legacy Cards and Character Sleeves — no journal required at any level of play.

---

## Product Structure

**The Core Box** contains the complete game system plus the **Midgard starter campaign** (*Shadows Over Järnvik*) — 5 scenario sleeves, 4 Realm Legacy Cards, 1 World Tree Legacy Card, realm-specific loot items, and the **grand campaign map** showing all 9 realms connected by Yggdrasil's branches.

**Realm Campaign Packs** are self-contained expansions, one per realm. Each pack contains:
- A set of Scenario Sleeves (number bounded by book-box capacity — see Phase 7)
- Realm Legacy Cards for that campaign
- Realm-specific loot shop items (equipment and consumables themed to that realm)
- Any realm-specific special rules or components
- A defined linking structure: standalone, sequential, or conditional (campaign designer's choice)

---

## The Grand Campaign

The grand campaign is the full arc across all 9 realms. The grand campaign map tracks which realms have been visited, completed, and how they connect.

### Realm Linking Types

Campaign designers choose how their realm connects to others:

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Standalone** | Can be played at any point, no prerequisites |
| **Sequential** | Unlocks only after a specific realm is completed |
| **Conditional** | Unlocks based on a specific World Tree Legacy Card outcome |

### The Grand Campaign Map

Included in the core box. Shows all 9 realms arranged around Yggdrasil's branches. Serves two functions:

**Navigation:** Tracks which realms have been visited, completed won or lost, and which are currently accessible. Each realm has a small tracking space — mark when entered, mark the outcome (won/lost).

**Linking:** Realm Campaign Packs may include a map overlay insert that prints route connections, showing which realms their campaign unlocks or requires. Players place the overlay on the relevant realm position.

**Physical design:** The map is printed on the third panel of the book-box unfold (the campaign panel). Dry-erase compatible so it can be reset between grand campaigns. See Phase 7.

## Hero Progression Across the Grand Campaign

### The Mastery Pool System

Heroes have two separate card pools that operate independently:

**Mastery Pool** — all ability cards ever unlocked across the grand campaign. This **never resets**. It grows permanently as heroes level up across all realm campaigns. It represents everything the hero has ever learned.

**Active Pool** — the cards actually played with in the current realm. Determined by current level. When a level grants a card unlock, the hero chooses any card from their Mastery Pool not already in their Active Pool.

This means:
- **Power level resets** each realm — each new world feels genuinely challenging
- **Class mastery grows** across the grand campaign — the hero becomes richer and more versatile over time
- **Card selection stays meaningful** — with a growing Mastery Pool, choosing which cards to bring into each realm and each Act is a constantly evolving decision

### Level Reset Between Realms

When heroes complete a realm campaign and begin the next, their level resets to **Level 3**.

At Level 3 heroes have:
- +2 Health (from Level 3 reward) already applied — they feel capable
- 2 card unlock slots available — choosing from their growing Mastery Pool
- Dice threshold NOT yet improved (Level 5) — the realm is genuinely challenging
- Signature ability NOT yet enhanced (Level 4) — something to earn early

The journey from Level 3 → 9 within a realm requires approximately 185 XP — achievable in a 6-8 scenario campaign on a strong run.

### The Mastery Pool Growth Arc

Each time a hero gains a card-unlock level (2, 4, 6, 8), two new cards are added to their Mastery Pool permanently. The pool grows across realm campaigns until the class maximum is reached.

| After Realm | Approx Level Reached | Cards Added | Mastery Pool Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starting Midgard | Level 1 | 8 starting cards | 8 |
| End of Midgard | Level 6 | +6 | 14 |
| End of Realm 2 | Level 8 | +4 | 18 |
| End of Realm 3 | Level 9 | +2 | 20 |
| Realm 4+ | Level 9 | 0 (maximum reached) | 16-20 (class max) |

**Full Mastery Pool is reached around Realm 3-4.** From that point the hero is complete — but the Level 3 reset and the item system ensure each realm still feels earned and challenging.

> **Note on small-pool classes:** The Ulfhednar has a maximum pool of 10 cards (smaller than other classes by design). They will complete their Mastery Pool faster — around Realm 2. Consider permanent cross-realm bonuses for small-pool classes once their pool is complete. See Phase 2 revision notes in DEV_PLAN.

### What Carries Forward Between Realms

| Element | Status |
|---|---|
| **Mastery Pool** (all ever-unlocked cards) | ✅ Permanent — never resets |
| **Active Pool** (current level's cards) | ✅ Carries — re-selected at Level 3 from Mastery Pool |
| **Loot Tokens** | ✅ Carries — individual, capped at 10 |
| **Equipped Items** | ✅ Carries — stored in Character Sleeve |
| **World Tree Legacy Cards** | ✅ Permanent — always in spine |
| **Hero Level** | ↩️ Resets to Level 3 |
| **Health** | ↩️ Resets to Level 3 maximum |
| **XP Tokens** | ↩️ Cleared — fresh XP track at Level 3 |
| **Realm Legacy Cards** | ❌ Archived in completed realm pack |

## Two-Tier Legacy Card System

### Realm Legacy Cards (6 slots per realm campaign)

Earned within a single realm campaign at **special story moments** — not automatically after every scenario. Campaign designers define exactly when they are earned:
- A specific Act 2 objective is achieved
- A significant narrative choice is made
- A named trigger condition is met (printed on the Scenario Sleeve)

**Design constraint:** Effects must apply broadly across multiple scenarios within the realm — not so specific they only fire once.

**Physical home:** The realm campaign pack's own Legacy sleeve. Archived back into the pack when the realm is complete.

### World Tree Legacy Cards (6 slots, permanent spine)

Earned only for the most significant cross-realm moments:
- Completing a realm campaign (won or lost side)
- Making a named grand campaign choice
- A specific trigger defined by the campaign designer

**Design constraint:** Effects must apply across multiple realms — no realm-specific references.

**Physical home:** 6 fixed slots in the book-box spine (on the campaign panel). Never removed. Later slot always wins on contradictions.

### Legacy Card Rules (Both Tiers)

- Read left to right within each tier at session start
- **Later slot wins** on any contradiction — World Tree cards override Realm cards on direct conflicts
- **15-word constraint** applies to all cards at both tiers
- Additive effects stack naturally; compounding effects are intentional
- Skip cards irrelevant to this scenario's location or type

## Between-Session Flow

After every scenario, in this order:

1. **Resolve Act 3** — apply win or loss outcome from the Scenario Sleeve
2. **Award Scenario XP** — 20 XP (win) or 10 XP (loss) + named consolation reward if loss
3. **Count Card XP** — total XP tokens earned during session, add to running total
4. **Level up** — if XP threshold reached, advance the character board slider and apply rewards. If a card unlock level was reached, choose a card from Mastery Pool to add to Active Pool.
5. **Spend Loot** — each hero spends Loot Tokens individually at the loot shop
6. **Equip items** — swap equipment if desired. Replaced items discarded.
7. **Recover Health** — all heroes restore Health to current level maximum
8. **Place Legacy Cards** — if a trigger condition was met this scenario, place the earned Legacy Card (Realm or World Tree tier) in the next available slot
9. **Prepare next scenario** — set up the Scenario Sleeve

### Between-Realm Flow

When a realm campaign is complete and heroes move to a new realm:

1. **Resolve final World Tree Legacy Card** — place won or lost side in next spine slot
2. **Archive Realm Legacy Cards** — return all realm cards to the completed campaign pack
3. **Reset hero levels to 3** — slide XP track back to Level 3 on each Character Board
4. **Confirm Mastery Pool** — note all cards ever unlocked. These remain available.
5. **Select Active Pool** — at Level 3, each hero selects their Active Pool from their Mastery Pool (as many cards as Level 3 allows for their class)
6. **Carry Loot and Equipment** — nothing changes here. Loot Tokens and equipped items persist.
7. **Load new campaign** — swap Scenario Sleeves, load realm-specific monster cards and tokens, check grand campaign map for any World Tree Legacy Card modifiers
8. **Read new realm's Scenario Sleeve** — begin

## Loot & Items

### Loot Tokens

Universal currency. Earned individually, spent individually. Maximum 10 per hero. Carries forward between scenarios and realm campaigns.

### Earning Loot

| Source | Amount | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Scenario Completion (Win) | 3 Loot | At Act 3 Resolution |
| Scenario Completion (Loss) | 1 Loot | At Act 3 Resolution |
| Objective Loot | 1-2 Loot | Mid-scenario, printed on Scenario Sleeve |
| Class Action Loot | 1-2 Loot | When a Loot-marked card action is triggered |

### The Item System

Items modify the hero's behaviour during play independently of which ability cards are selected. Purchased with Loot Tokens between scenarios.

**Three equipment slots per hero (Weapon / Armour / Relic):**
- One item per slot
- Equipping a new item in an occupied slot discards the old one
- Swapping between scenarios only — never mid-session
- Items stored in Character Sleeve

**Realm-specific items:** Each Realm Campaign Pack introduces new items to the loot shop for that realm's duration — thematically tied to the realm's materials and magic.

See `05_progression.md` for the full base loot shop listing.

## Campaign Design Guidelines

These principles apply to all realm campaign designers:

**Scenario count:** Bounded by book-box physical capacity (see Phase 7). Target 4-6 scenarios per realm campaign.

**Legacy Card triggers:** Earned at special moments, not automatically. Each trigger must feel earned — a significant choice, a dramatic objective, or a notable outcome. Aim for 3-5 Realm Legacy Cards per campaign.

**World Tree Legacy Card:** Every completed realm campaign earns one World Tree Legacy Card. The won and lost sides must be broadly applicable — no realm-specific references. Effects should feel like a permanent scar or blessing on the heroes' grand journey.

**Boss design:** Bosses carry no threshold text on their Monster Cards. All threshold behaviour is defined on the Scenario Sleeve. Bosses can appear in multiple scenarios — they escape rather than die, and may return in later scenarios as an escalating recurring threat.

**Linking structure:** Define clearly whether your realm is standalone, sequential, or conditional. Include a map overlay insert if your realm requires a specific predecessor.

**Difficulty curve:** Scenarios should escalate within the realm campaign. The finale should feel earned — incorporate Legacy Card states from earlier in the campaign where possible (as demonstrated in Scenario 5 of the Midgard campaign).
