# Shadows of the Nine Realms — Character Classes

Six unique classes offer diverse playstyles. Each class has a unique card pool, defined hand size, signature ability, and progression path from levels 1-9.

## Class Design Principles

- **Pool size** and **hand size** express class personality — not just card effects
- **Card selection** at each Act transition creates genuine sacrifice decisions
- **Items** provide variety across the grand campaign — not additional cards
- No two classes should feel similar at the table
- Each class has 3-4 **XP-marked actions** and 1-2 **Loot-marked actions** with clear narrative logic

## ✅ The Nidstang

*"I raise the curse-pole. Let doom find the unworthy."*

Masters of the Nidstang — the curse-pole — these dark practitioners wield forbidden magic rooted in authentic Norse níð tradition. They excel at debuffs, damage over time, and isolating enemies through accumulated doom.

- **Role:** Debuff / Control / Damage-over-Time
- **Health:** 8 *(fragile — curses from range, needs protection)*
- **Speed:** 4
- **Pool Size:** 6 cards (4 starting + 2 unlockable)
- **Hand Size:** Class-defined *(pending balance pass)*

### The Curse Token System

The Nidstang's identity runs through **Curse Tokens** — small dark wooden discs placed on Monster Cards (not hex positions). Curses are personal. An enemy carries its curse wherever it moves.

| Curse Tokens | State | Effect on Enemy |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Marked** | -1 to all attack rolls |
| 2 | **Níðingr** | -1 attack AND rolls at Disadvantage |
| 3 | **Doomed** | -1 attack, Disadvantage, AND 1 damage at Monster Phase start |

- Maximum **3 Curse Tokens** per enemy
- Tokens persist until the enemy is defeated or the scenario ends
- When a cursed enemy is defeated, the Nidstang earns **1 XP** ⭐

### Signature — The Nídstǫng Pole

> *"I plant the curse-pole. The runes are spoken. Let doom find the unworthy."*

**Base (Levels 1-3):** Once per scenario, spend a Bottom Action to plant the Nídstǫng Pole on your current hex. While it stands:
- All enemies within 2 hexes begin their activation with 1 Curse Token (if they have none)
- The pole stays until end of Act OR until the Nidstang moves more than 3 hexes away

**Level 4 Enhancement — Rune Inscription:**
When planting the pole, carve one Rune. Choose one:
- **Rune of Weakness** — enemies within 2 hexes deal -1 damage
- **Rune of Fear** — enemies within 2 hexes prefer not to move toward other enemies
- **Rune of Rot** — enemies within 2 hexes gain Curse Tokens twice per activation

**Level 8 Mastery — The Living Pole:**
The pole is no longer fixed. The 2-hex cursed zone follows the Nidstang everywhere. The pole never falls when they move.

### Dice Table

| Roll | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | **Glancing Curse** — Deal 1 damage OR place 1 Curse Token on target |
| 4-6 | **Weak Hex** — Half damage, no curse placed |
| 7-14 | **Standard Hex** — Full damage AND place 1 Curse Token on target |
| 15-19 | **Strong Hex** — Full damage, place 1 Curse Token AND spread 1 token to adjacent enemy |
| 20 | **Critical Doom** — Double damage, place 2 Curse Tokens on target, gain 2 Fate Tokens |

At Standard Hit and above, the Nidstang always does two things simultaneously — damage and curse. This is their core mechanical identity.

### Starting Cards

**Card 1 — Níð Chant** *(Initiative 2)*
- **TOP:** Place 2 Curse Tokens on any enemy within 3 hexes (no attack roll) ⭐ *XP icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Move up to 3 hexes

*Low initiative = strong Rest card (removes 2 Exhaustion). TOP sacrifices attack entirely for pure curse throughput.*

---

**Card 2 — Rune Strike** *(Initiative 5)*
- **TOP:** Attack one enemy. On any hit, place 1 additional Curse Token beyond the Dice Table result ⭐ *XP icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Transfer 1 Curse Token from any enemy to an adjacent enemy

*Transfer mechanic is the tactical control element — routing curse tokens to priority targets.*

---

**Card 3 — Spit of the Pole** *(Initiative 7)*
- **TOP:** Attack all enemies within 1 hex simultaneously (one roll, applied to all). Place 1 Curse Token on each hit enemy 💰 *Loot icon — if 2+ enemies hit, gain 1 Loot (the terror of multiple curses has tradeable value)*
- **BOTTOM:** Remove all Curse Tokens from one enemy — that enemy suffers damage equal to the number of tokens removed ⭐ *XP icon*

*BOTTOM is the "detonate" — let curses stack, then release as burst damage.*

---

**Card 4 — Walk of Shame** *(Initiative 9)*
- **TOP:** Attack one enemy. On Strong or Critical Hit, force that enemy to move 2 hexes away (fear — battlefield repositioning) 💰 *Loot icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Move through enemy hexes without triggering attacks. All enemies within 1 hex of your path gain 1 Curse Token

*High initiative = weak Rest card, but 9 Exhaustion removed if Rested with — powerful tension.*

### Unlockable Cards

**Card 5 — The Horse Skull** *(Unlocked Level 2, Initiative 4)* ⭐ *XP icon*
- **TOP:** Plant the Nídstǫng Pole AND make a standard attack this turn (normally mutually exclusive)
- **BOTTOM:** All Níðingr (2-token) enemies within 3 hexes are Stunned until their next activation

**Card 6 — Corruption Spreads** *(Unlocked Level 6, Initiative 6)*
- **TOP:** Choose one Doomed (3-token) enemy — place 1 Curse Token on every enemy within 2 hexes of it ⭐ *XP icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Gain Invisible until your next turn

### Design Notes
- Curse Tokens are stored in the Nidstang's Character Sleeve
- The "detonate" on Spit of the Pole (BOTTOM) counts as fulfilled for XP purposes — the curse reached its conclusion
- The Nídstǫng Pole's cursed zone interacts with ISOLATIONIST Monster AI keyword — enemies near the pole may behave erratically, which is thematically perfect
- Health of 8 means Strained state is a real risk — positioning is a skill expression
