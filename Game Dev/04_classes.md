# Shadows of the Nine Realms — Character Classes

Six unique classes offer diverse playstyles. Each class has a unique card pool, defined hand size, signature ability, and progression path from levels 1-9.

> ⚠️ **Phase 2 note:** All classes are first iterations. Balance passes, card tuning, and pool size validation happen in Phase 6 playtesting.

## Class Design Principles

- **Pool size** and **hand size** express class personality — not just card effects
- **TOP + BOTTOM pairing** is the core round decision — which TOP do I want, which card's TOP am I willing to sacrifice for its BOTTOM?
- **Items** provide variety across the grand campaign — not additional cards
- No two classes should feel similar at the table
- Each class has 3-4 **XP-marked actions** and 1-2 **Loot-marked actions** with clear narrative logic

## Class Pool Overview

| Class | Starting Pool | Max Pool (Level 9) | Hand Size | Identity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Einherjar | 8 | 16 | 6 | Consistent — positional complexity, not card complexity |
| Nidstang | 8 | 16 | 5 | Setup — pool variety prevents identical curse strategies |
| Valkyrie | 8 | 16 | 4 | Adaptive — small hand forces hard choices each Act |
| Seiðr | 8 | 16 | 5 | Reactive — needs the right tool at the right moment |
| Ulfhednar | 6 | 10 | 6 | Two modes — small pool reflects human/beast duality |
| Skald | 8 | 16 | 4 | Performance — always choosing what song fits this moment |

> ⚠️ Pool sizes and hand sizes are first iteration estimates. Validated in Phase 6.

## ✅ The Einherjar

*"Stand firm, shield-brothers! The Einherjar do not yield!"*

Fallen warriors chosen by Odin to feast in Valhalla until Ragnarök. They train daily, wounds healing each night, readying for the final battle. The shield wall that protects the realms.

- **Role:** Tank / Defender
- **Health:** 14 *(highest in the game)*
- **Speed:** 3 *(slowest — movement is a significant decision)*
- **Pool Size:** 8 starting → 16 at Level 9
- **Hand Size:** 6 from pool

### The Hold Ground System

The Einherjar's unique mechanic. At the start of their turn, if they did not move last round, they gain 1 **Hold Ground token.** Three small wooden discs track the count (stored in Character Sleeve).

| Tokens | State | Bonus |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Moving | No bonus |
| 1 | **Planted** | Shield 1 at start of each turn |
| 2 | **Rooted** | Shield 1 + adjacent allies reduce incoming damage by 1 |
| 3 | **Immovable** | Shield 2 + adjacent allies reduce damage by 1 + +2 to all attack rolls |

If the Einherjar moves for **any reason** — willingly or forced — all tokens are lost immediately. Positioning decisions are high stakes.

### Signature — Skjaldborg (Shield Wall)

**Base (Levels 1-3):** Once per turn, extend shield to one adjacent ally. That ally gains Shield 1 until start of Einherjar's next turn. Requires Planted (1+ tokens).

**Level 4 Enhancement — The Wall Holds:**
Shield extension now covers **all** adjacent allies simultaneously.

**Level 8 Mastery — Unbroken Line:**
While Immovable (3 tokens), adjacent allies **cannot be forcibly moved** by any monster ability.

### Dice Table

| Roll | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | **Glancing Blow** — Deal 1 damage AND gain 1 Hold Ground token |
| 4-6 | **Weak Hit** — Half damage, no bonus |
| 7-14 | **Standard Hit** — Full damage + Shield 1 until start of next turn |
| 15-19 | **Strong Hit** — Full damage + push target 1 hex away + Shield 1 |
| 20 | **Critical Blow** — Double damage, push 2 hexes, Shield 2 until next turn, gain 1 Fate Token |

Even Glancing Blows reward staying put — fail-forward is baked into the class identity.

### Starting Cards (8)

**Card 1 — Plant Your Feet** *(Initiative 1)*
- **TOP:** Gain 2 Hold Ground tokens immediately ⭐ *XP icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Move up to 2 hexes

*Lowest initiative — the Einherjar always goes last, and is always exactly where they need to be.*

---

**Card 2 — Shield Slam** *(Initiative 4)*
- **TOP:** Attack one adjacent enemy. On any hit, enemy is Stunned until their next activation ⭐ *XP icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Move up to 2 hexes and position between an ally and nearest enemy (lose all Hold Ground tokens)

---

**Card 3 — Hold the Line** *(Initiative 3)*
- **TOP:** Until start of next turn, the first attack against any adjacent ally is redirected to the Einherjar instead ⭐ *XP icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Remove 2 Exhaustion Tokens from any adjacent ally 💰 *Loot icon*

---

**Card 4 — Valhalla Calls** *(Initiative 6)*
- **TOP:** Attack one enemy within 2 hexes. If Rooted or Immovable (2-3 tokens), deal +3 additional damage 💰 *Loot icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Gain Immovable status until end of turn — cannot be forcibly moved by any monster ability this round

---

**Card 5 — Brace** *(Initiative 2)*
- **TOP:** Gain 3 Hold Ground tokens AND Shield 3 this round — pure defensive commitment
- **BOTTOM:** Grant Shield 1 to all adjacent allies until start of your next turn ⭐ *XP icon*

---

**Card 6 — Shield Bash** *(Initiative 5)*
- **TOP:** Attack one adjacent enemy — push them 2 hexes away
- **BOTTOM:** Move up to 3 hexes without losing Hold Ground tokens this turn (the Einherjar surges forward briefly, shield raised)

*Shield Bash BOTTOM is the exception — the only card that allows movement without penalty, once per use.*

---

**Card 7 — Óðinn's Chosen** *(Initiative 3)*
- **TOP:** Reactive — when an ally would be Incapacitated this round, immediately move to their hex and take the triggering hit instead ⭐ *XP icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Gain 1 Hold Ground token AND remove 1 Exhaustion Token from yourself

---

**Card 8 — Defy the Giant** *(Initiative 7)*
- **TOP:** Deal damage equal to 2× the number of Hold Ground tokens currently held (minimum 2). No attack roll needed. 💰 *Loot icon*
- **BOTTOM:** All enemies adjacent to you must make a move action away from you — they cannot attack this Monster Phase activation (fear effect)

*High initiative — useful as a Rest card (removes 7 Exhaustion). The BOTTOM is the class's crowd control option.*

### Unlockable Cards (+2 per unlock, levels 2/4/6/8)

> ⚠️ 8 additional cards to be designed in Phase 2. Each should introduce new tactical variations on holding ground, protecting allies, or punishing encroaching enemies. Examples:
> - **The Fallen Rise** — survive at 1 Health when reduced to 0 (thematically: Einherjar have died before)
> - **Break Them** — attack all adjacent enemies simultaneously, push each 1 hex
> - **Wall of Spears** — adjacent allies gain +2 to attack rolls this round (the shield wall advances)
> - **Iron Will** — negate all Exhaustion gained this round

### Design Notes
- Hold Ground tokens: 3 small wooden discs per Einherjar, stored in Character Sleeve
- **Card 5 BOTTOM** (grant Shield 1 to all adjacent allies) interacts with Break Them unlockable — powerful at high levels
- **Speed 3** means Scenario Sleeve designers should ensure not all objectives require movement
- Wolf Pelt armour item is particularly strong on the Einherjar — they take hits constantly

## ✅ The Nidstang

*"I raise the curse-pole. Let doom find the unworthy."*

Masters of the Nidstang — the curse-pole — dark practitioners of níð, the Norse tradition of ritual humiliation and cursing. Setup class — patience rewarded.

- **Role:** Debuff / Control / Damage-over-Time
- **Health:** 8 *(fragile — curses from range, needs protection)*
- **Speed:** 4
- **Pool Size:** 8 starting → 16 at Level 9
- **Hand Size:** 5 from pool

### The Curse Token System

**Curse Tokens** (dark wooden discs) placed on Monster Cards — not hex positions. Curses are personal.

| Curse Tokens | State | Effect on Enemy |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Marked** | -1 to all attack rolls |
| 2 | **Níðingr** | -1 attack AND rolls at Disadvantage |
| 3 | **Doomed** | -1 attack, Disadvantage, AND 1 damage at Monster Phase start |

- Maximum 3 tokens per enemy
- Tokens persist until enemy is defeated or scenario ends
- Cursed enemy defeated → Nidstang earns 1 XP ⭐

### Signature — The Nídstǫng Pole

**Base (Levels 1-3):** Once per scenario, spend a Bottom Action to plant the Pole on current hex. While standing:
- All enemies within 2 hexes begin their activation with 1 Curse Token (if they have none)
- Pole stays until end of Act OR Nidstang moves more than 3 hexes away

**Level 4 Enhancement — Rune Inscription:**
Carve one Rune when planting. Choose:
- **Rune of Weakness** — enemies within 2 hexes deal -1 damage
- **Rune of Fear** — enemies within 2 hexes prefer not to move toward other enemies
- **Rune of Rot** — enemies within 2 hexes gain Curse Tokens twice per activation

**Level 8 Mastery — The Living Pole:**
The 2-hex cursed zone follows the Nidstang everywhere. Pole never falls when they move.

### Dice Table

| Roll | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | **Glancing Curse** — Deal 1 damage OR place 1 Curse Token on target |
| 4-6 | **Weak Hex** — Half damage, no curse placed |
| 7-14 | **Standard Hex** — Full damage AND place 1 Curse Token on target |
| 15-19 | **Strong Hex** — Full damage, place 1 Curse Token AND spread 1 to adjacent enemy |
| 20 | **Critical Doom** — Double damage, place 2 Curse Tokens, gain 2 Fate Tokens |

### Starting Cards (8)

**Card 1 — Níð Chant** *(Initiative 2)*
- **TOP:** Place 2 Curse Tokens on any enemy within 3 hexes (no attack roll) ⭐ *XP icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Move up to 3 hexes

**Card 2 — Rune Strike** *(Initiative 5)*
- **TOP:** Attack one enemy. On any hit, place 1 additional Curse Token beyond Dice Table result ⭐ *XP icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Transfer 1 Curse Token from any enemy to an adjacent enemy

**Card 3 — Spit of the Pole** *(Initiative 7)*
- **TOP:** Attack all enemies within 1 hex (one roll, applied to all). Place 1 Curse Token on each hit 💰 *Loot icon — if 2+ enemies hit, gain 1 Loot*
- **BOTTOM:** Remove all Curse Tokens from one enemy — that enemy suffers damage equal to tokens removed ⭐ *XP icon*

**Card 4 — Walk of Shame** *(Initiative 9)*
- **TOP:** Attack one enemy. On Strong or Critical Hit, force enemy to move 2 hexes away 💰 *Loot icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Move through enemy hexes without triggering attacks. All enemies within 1 hex of path gain 1 Curse Token

**Card 5 — Ritual Humiliation** *(Initiative 3)*
- **TOP:** Choose one enemy — remove their Secondary keyword for this round (they use only their Primary, or CLOSEST fallback if no Primary is valid)  ⭐ *XP icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Move up to 2 hexes AND place 1 Curse Token on nearest enemy

**Card 6 — Corroding Fate** *(Initiative 6)*
- **TOP:** All Marked (1-token) enemies within 3 hexes gain a second Curse Token simultaneously
- **BOTTOM:** Gain Invisible until start of your next turn 💰 *Loot icon*

**Card 7 — Seething Curse** *(Initiative 4)*
- **TOP:** Attack one enemy. If they are already Níðingr (2 tokens), deal double damage ⭐ *XP icon*
- **BOTTOM:** Move 1 hex AND extend the Nídstǫng Pole's reach by 1 hex until end of round

**Card 8 — Pole's Shadow** *(Initiative 1)*
- **TOP:** Place the Nídstǫng Pole on any hex within 3 hexes of you (not just your current hex)
- **BOTTOM:** Remove 2 Curse Tokens from any enemy — you absorb the curse energy. Gain 1 Fate Token per token removed

### Unlockable Cards (+2 per unlock, levels 2/4/6/8)

> ⚠️ 8 additional cards to be designed in Phase 2. Include:
> - **The Horse Skull** — plant pole AND attack in same turn
> - **Corruption Spreads** — Doomed enemy radiates 1 token to all enemies within 2 hexes
> - Further curse manipulation and control options

### Design Notes
- Curse Tokens stored in Character Sleeve (dark wooden discs, distinct from all other tokens)
- "Detonate" on Spit of the Pole BOTTOM counts as fulfilled for XP purposes
- The Nídstǫng Pole's cursed zone interacts interestingly with ISOLATIONIST Monster AI
