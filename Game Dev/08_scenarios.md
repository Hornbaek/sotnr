# Shadows of the Nine Realms — Scenarios

Each scenario uses a Scenario Sleeve (front/back) that slides over the map half of the book-box. The sleeve defines everything needed to run the scenario — terrain, objectives, Act triggers, transitions, and Legacy Card instructions.

---

## Scenario Sleeve Format

**Front Side:**
- **Zone 1 — Header:** Name, location, difficulty, players, monsters
- **Zone 2 — Hex Map Overlay:** Terrain, starting positions, spawns, objective markers
- **Zone 3 — Act 1:** Trigger type, round count, Victorious outcome, Besieged outcome
- **Zone 6 — Special Rules:** Scenario-wide rules

**Back Side:**
- **[Act 1 → Act 2 Transition Strip]:** Suppression icons (if any)
- **Zone 4 — Act 2:** Trigger type, round count, Victorious outcome, Besieged outcome
- **[Act 2 → Act 3 Transition Strip]:** Suppression icons (if any)
- **Zone 5 — Act 3 Resolution:** Win text, loss text, consolation reward, Legacy Card instruction

**Act Trigger Types:**
- **Type A (Objective):** Act ends when a condition is met
- **Type B (Round Count):** Act ends after X rounds regardless
- **Type C (Survival):** Act ends after X rounds — outcome depends on whether a condition was avoided

Every Act has one primary trigger and one fallback. Every transition resolves into exactly two outcomes: **Victorious** or **Besieged**.

# THE MIDGARD STARTER CAMPAIGN
## *Shadows Over Järnvik*

*Something is wrong in the region around the village of Järnvik. The creatures of the dark are stirring — more boldly, more frequently, and seemingly drawn toward the same place. The heroes arrive as strangers. They may leave as legends.*

### Campaign Structure
5 scenarios, linear with one meaningful branch at Scenario 1.

```
S1 (Huldra) ──Won──► S2 ──► S3 ──► S4 ──► S5 (Huldra as optional ally)
           └──Lost──► S2 ──► S3 ──► S4 ──► S5 (Huldra as enemy)
```

### Campaign Legacy Cards Summary

| Scenario | Won Side | Lost Side |
|---|---|---|
| S1 | In forest scenarios, the Huldra does not spawn as enemy | In forest scenarios, add 1 Huldra token at Act 2 start |
| S2 | Draugr deal −1 damage in all scenarios | Draugr gain Shield 1 in all scenarios |
| S3 | Heroes begin each session with 1 bonus Fate Token | All heroes begin each session with 1 Exhaustion Token |
| S4 | Negotiation actions roll with Advantage | Troll attacks deal +1 damage in all scenarios |
| S5 | Järnvik endures — each hero gains 3 Loot entering next realm | Järnvik falls — each hero begins next realm with 1 Exhaustion Token |

## Scenario 1 — The Hollow of the Huldra

*"They say she lures the lonely into the deep woods. None who follow return the same."*

---

### FRONT SIDE

**Zone 1 — Header**
- **Location:** Järnvik Forest, Midgard
- **Difficulty:** ⭐ Introductory
- **Players:** 1-4
- **Monsters:** Huldra ×1, Myling ×2

**Zone 2 — Hex Map**
```
Terrain: Deep forest. Dense trees create obstacle hexes in a rough ring
around a central hollow. Three water hexes (a stream) run diagonally
across the lower half.

Starting hexes:  Village edge — top row, 2-4 adjacent hexes
Huldra spawn:    Central hollow hex
Myling spawns:   Two forest hexes, mid-map, one either side of stream
Objective hex:   The hollow (centre map) — circle marker
```

**Zone 3 — Act 1**
*"The forest is quiet. Too quiet. The Huldra knows you are coming."*

- **Primary trigger (Type A):** Act 1 ends when any hero reaches the objective hex
- **Fallback (Type B):** Act 1 ends after Round 4

**Victorious:** *"You reach the hollow. The Huldra turns — her hollow back revealed. She is waiting."*
→ Act 2 begins with Huldra stationary (does not move her first activation). Heroes declare Eliminate or Negotiate approach.

**Besieged:** *"The Mylings drove you back. The Huldra has had time to prepare."*
→ Act 2 begins with Huldra having moved 2 hexes toward heroes. Spawn a third Myling at the hollow.

**Zone 6 — Special Rules**
- **Forest Lure:** At the start of each round, the hero furthest from any ally must roll D20 (8+) or move 1 hex toward the Huldra.
- **The Stream:** Heroes who end their turn on a water hex may remove 1 Exhaustion Token.

---

### BACK SIDE

**[Act 1 → Act 2 Transition Strip]**
No suppression icons — normal recovery and hand reset applies.

---

**Zone 4 — Act 2**
*"Now you see her clearly. The hollow back, the cow's tail. And still — she has not attacked."*

**ELIMINATE PATH:**
- **Primary trigger (Type A):** Huldra reduced to 3 HP or below — she breaks and flees
- **Fallback (Type B):** Round 5

Victorious: *"She screams — not rage but grief — and dissolves into the trees. The hollow is empty."*
Besieged: *"She laughs. You have given her what she wanted — a reason to fight back."*

**NEGOTIATE PATH:**
- Huldra does not activate as a monster this Act
- Heroes must place 3 Trust tokens on the objective hex — each requires a hero to spend a Bottom Action adjacent to the Huldra
- **Primary trigger (Type A):** 3 Trust tokens placed — OR — Huldra takes any damage (negotiation collapses, switch to Eliminate path immediately)
- **Fallback (Type B):** Round 4

Victorious: *"She speaks. The missing hunters followed willingly — they are not dead. They are changed."*
Besieged: *"The moment your blade was drawn, something in her eyes closed. Switch to Eliminate — Act 2 continues."*

**[Act 2 → Act 3 Transition Strip]**
No suppression icons.

---

**Zone 5 — Act 3 Resolution**

**Win (Eliminate):** *"The Huldra is gone. The hunters stumble from the trees — hollow-eyed but alive."*
- 3 Loot each + 20 XP. Legacy Card: won side.

**Win (Negotiate):** *"The hunters return. The Huldra vanishes. She may remember this."*
- 3 Loot each + 1 Fate Token each + 20 XP. Legacy Card: won side.

**Loss:** *"She escapes. The hunters are gone. The villagers bar their doors."*
- 1 Loot each + 10 XP.
- Consolation: *"One hunter staggers back alone. He gives you his silver armband. Gain 2 Loot each."*
- Legacy Card: lost side.

**Legacy Card — Scenario 1**

| Won | Lost |
|---|---|
| In forest scenarios, the Huldra does not spawn as enemy. | In forest scenarios, add 1 Huldra token at Act 2 start. |

## Scenario 2 — The Waking Mound

*"The burial ground has burst from within. The jarl's fury is older than the village."*

---

### FRONT SIDE

**Zone 1 — Header**
- **Location:** Burial fields east of Järnvik, Midgard
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐ Standard
- **Players:** 1-4
- **Monsters:** Draugr ×3 (one is the Jarl — see Special Rules), Myling ×2

**Zone 2 — Hex Map**
```
Terrain: Open burial ground at night. Scattered grave-mounds create
elevated hexes (higher ground). Broken stone wall along left edge —
obstacle hexes. Central mound: 3 elevated hexes, surrounded by
difficult terrain (disturbed earth).

Starting hexes:  Southern edge, village road — 2-4 adjacent hexes
Jarl Draugr:     Atop central mound (marked token)
Draugr ×2:       Mid-map ground level, flanking
Myling ×2:       Northern corners, near the wall
Objective hexes: Three seal hexes — mound entrance + two flanking
```

**Zone 3 — Act 1**
*"The mound has burst from within. Stop them before they reach the village."*

- **Primary trigger (Type C):** Act 1 ends after Round 3. Outcome depends on whether any Draugr reached the village edge (bottom map row).
- **Fallback:** Round 3 always ends Act 1 — no extension.

**Victorious:** *"You held the line. The burial ground is still contained."*
→ Jarl Draugr fights at base stats in Act 2.

**Besieged:** *"One broke through. The screaming in the village has already started."*
→ One additional Myling spawns at village edge hex. Jarl Draugr gains +1 damage for the rest of the scenario.

**Zone 6 — Special Rules**
- **The Jarl Draugr:** Marked token. +2 Health (total 8). Uses ELITE keyword instead of ISOLATIONIST. Does not leave the mound hex until Act 2.
- **Higher Ground:** Elevated mound hexes grant Advantage to any attacker on them — hero or monster.
- **Darkness:** Heroes cannot see beyond 4 hexes. Ranged attacks beyond 4 hexes roll at Disadvantage.

---

### BACK SIDE

**[Act 1 → Act 2 Transition Strip]**
No suppression icons — normal recovery and hand reset applies.

---

**Zone 4 — Act 2**
*"The jarl descends from his mound. He remembers the faces of those who wronged him. Yours are new faces. That makes it worse."*

- **Primary trigger (Type A):** All three seal hexes activated (hero spends Bottom Action on each) AND Jarl Draugr reduced to 2 HP or below. Both conditions required.
- **Fallback (Type B):** Round 5

**Victorious:** *"The seal holds. The jarl lets out a sound that has no name in any living language — and sinks back into the earth."*

**Besieged:** *"The seal is incomplete. The jarl withdraws — not defeated, merely satisfied."*
→ All surviving Draugr removed from map.

**[Act 2 → Act 3 Transition Strip]**
No suppression icons.

---

**Zone 5 — Act 3 Resolution**

**Win:** *"The mound is sealed. The burial ground is quiet again. For now."*
- 3 Loot each + 20 XP.
- Bonus: One hero (player choice) finds the jarl's grave goods — gain 1 equipment item from loot shop at half cost.
- Legacy Card: won side.

**Loss:** *"The jarl returns to his mound unbroken. Something has shifted in Järnvik's relationship with its dead."*
- 1 Loot each + 10 XP.
- Consolation: *"A gravedigger presses silver into your hands, grateful you tried. Gain 2 Loot each."*
- Legacy Card: lost side.

**Legacy Card — Scenario 2**

| Won | Lost |
|---|---|
| Draugr deal −1 damage in all scenarios. | Draugr gain Shield 1 in all scenarios. |

## Scenario 3 — What Rides the Night

*"She sits upon your chest while you sleep. You cannot move. You cannot scream."*

---

### FRONT SIDE

**Zone 1 — Header**
- **Location:** Järnvik village, Midgard — at night
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ Challenging
- **Players:** 1-4
- **Monsters:** Mara ×1, Myling ×3

**Zone 2 — Hex Map**
```
Terrain: Village at night. Buildings create obstacle hexes forming
rough corridors. Three Hearth hexes (lit fires outside buildings)
scattered across map — provide light. Four Sleeper hexes marked
inside buildings — represent sleeping villagers.

Starting hexes:  Village centre, near a Hearth hex — 2-4 adjacent
Mara spawn:      Far building, hidden (see Special Rules)
Myling spawns:   Village edges, one per quadrant except heroes' start

Objective hexes: The four Sleeper hexes — must be protected
```

**Zone 3 — Act 1**
*"The village is asleep. Something moves between the buildings."*

- **Primary trigger (Type C):** Act 1 ends after Round 3. Outcome depends on whether any Sleeper hex has been visited by the Mara.
- **Fallback:** Round 3 always ends Act 1.

**Victorious:** *"The Mara has not yet fed. The villagers stir uneasily but wake."*
→ All four Sleeper hexes remain active in Act 2.

**Besieged:** *"One sleeper does not wake. The Mara has fed — and grown stronger."*
→ One Sleeper hex is removed. The Mara gains +1 to all Exhaustion Token applications for the rest of the scenario.

**Zone 6 — Special Rules**
- **Hidden Mara:** The Mara's starting hex is not revealed until she activates for the first time or a hero moves within 2 hexes of her. Place her token face-down.
- **Sleeper hexes:** If the Mara ends her activation on a Sleeper hex, that sleeper is lost — remove the hex marker. If all four are lost, the scenario ends immediately as a Besieged Act 3.
- **Hearth hexes:** Heroes on a Hearth hex cannot be targeted by the Mara's movement effects (the firelight breaks her hold). The Mara will not move onto a Hearth hex.
- **Dawn:** If heroes survive Round 4 of Act 2, dawn breaks — the Mara must flee. Act 2 ends immediately as Victorious.

---

### BACK SIDE

**[Act 1 → Act 2 Transition Strip]**
🌙 **Act 1→2 Exhaustion recovery SUPPRESSED.** The night deepens — there is no rest to be had.
Hand reset applies normally.

---

**Zone 4 — Act 2**
*"She is real. She is here. And the night is far from over."*

- **Primary trigger (Type A):** Mara reduced to 3 HP or below — she retreats before dawn
- **Secondary win condition:** Survive until Round 4 (dawn breaks — see Special Rules)
- **Fallback (Type B):** Round 6 — Besieged outcome regardless

**Victorious (Mara driven off):** *"She screams — a sound like a night terror given voice — and tears away into the dark. The survivors will remember this dawn."*

**Victorious (survived to dawn):** *"The first grey light breaks the horizon. Whatever she is, she cannot face it. She dissolves before your eyes."*

**Besieged:** *"She retreats on her own terms, satisfied. Three sleepers lost. The village will not be the same."*

**[Act 2 → Act 3 Transition Strip]**
No suppression icons.

---

**Zone 5 — Act 3 Resolution**

**Win:** *"The village wakes to a grey, shaken dawn. Those who survived the night will never speak of what they dreamed."*
- 3 Loot each + 20 XP.
- Bonus: The village elder gives each hero a carved protection rune. Each hero gains 1 Relic equipment item (Rune-Carved Pendant) at no cost — limit one per hero.
- Legacy Card: won side.

**Loss:** *"The village survives — barely. Four sleepers are gone or broken. The rest look at you with something between gratitude and accusation."*
- 1 Loot each + 10 XP.
- Consolation: *"The village healer tends your wounds through the night. Each hero removes all Exhaustion Tokens."*
- Legacy Card: lost side.

**Legacy Card — Scenario 3**

| Won | Lost |
|---|---|
| Heroes begin each session with 1 bonus Fate Token. | All heroes begin each session with 1 Exhaustion Token. |

## Scenario 4 — The Mountain Road

*"The pass has been blocked since the first snow. Something is keeping it that way."*

---

### FRONT SIDE

**Zone 1 — Header**
- **Location:** Hrafnberg Pass, Midgard — mountain road north of Järnvik
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ Challenging
- **Players:** 1-4
- **Monsters:** Troll ×1, Ice Wight ×2

**Zone 2 — Hex Map**
```
Terrain: Mountain pass. Rocky cliffs form obstacle hexes along both
edges. The pass narrows at the centre — a chokepoint of 2 hexes wide.
Elevated rocky outcroppings on both sides grant higher ground.
Deep snow covers the lower third of the map — difficult terrain.
A shrine hex sits at the far (north) end of the map — the objective.

Starting hexes:  Southern entry — 2-4 adjacent hexes in the snow
Troll spawn:     Chokepoint — the 2 centre hexes (he blocks the pass)
Ice Wight ×2:    Rocky outcroppings, elevated, one either side
Objective hex:   The shrine (far north end) — marked with a rune icon
```

**Zone 3 — Act 1**
*"The pass narrows ahead. The Troll has made himself comfortable blocking it. He has been here long enough to name the rocks."*

Heroes declare their approach before Act 1 begins. This is the only scenario where the approach is chosen before seeing the board state.

**THREE APPROACHES — choose one:**

**FIGHT:** Heroes attack the Troll directly. Normal combat rules. Ice Wights activate normally.
- **Trigger (Type A):** Troll reduced to 5 HP or below — he retreats and Act 1 ends
- **Fallback (Type B):** Round 4

**SNEAK:** Heroes attempt to bypass the Troll without engaging. Heroes must reach the chokepoint hexes without the Troll activating on them. If the Troll activates on a hero, switch to FIGHT immediately.
- Heroes move at half Speed (careful movement)
- **Trigger (Type A):** All heroes reach the far side of the chokepoint without triggering Troll activation
- **Fallback (Type B):** Round 3 — if not all heroes through, switch to FIGHT for remaining rounds

**BARGAIN:** Heroes offer the Troll something of value to stand aside. One hero must reach the chokepoint and spend 3 consecutive Bottom Actions there (the negotiation) without being attacked. Costs 3 Loot from that hero (the payment).
- Ice Wights still activate normally — the Troll's truce doesn't extend to them
- **Trigger (Type A):** 3 consecutive Bottom Actions completed by the same hero while adjacent to Troll
- **Fallback (Type B):** Round 4 — if not completed, Troll attacks, switch to FIGHT

**Victorious (any approach):** *"The chokepoint is behind you. The shrine is ahead."*
→ Act 2 begins with the Troll removed from the map. Ice Wights remain.

**Besieged (any approach):** *"The pass is not yet clear. You push forward battered."*
→ Act 2 begins with the Troll still active but moved 2 hexes south (away from shrine). Each hero gains 1 Exhaustion Token.

**Zone 6 — Special Rules**
- **Troll and Sunlight:** The shrine emits faint divine light. Any Troll on a hex within 2 hexes of the shrine rolls at Disadvantage on all attacks.
- **Deep Snow:** The lower third of the map (starting zone and two rows above) is difficult terrain for heroes. The Troll ignores it.
- **Ice Wight Terrain:** Each Ice Wight's activated hex becomes difficult terrain — the pass grows more treacherous as the fight continues.

---

### BACK SIDE

**[Act 1 → Act 2 Transition Strip]**
No suppression icons — normal recovery and hand reset applies.

---

**Zone 4 — Act 2**
*"The shrine is visible now. Whatever has been pulling the darkness toward Järnvik — it passes through here."*

- **Primary trigger (Type A):** Any hero reaches the shrine hex and spends a Top Action there (reading the runes)
- **Fallback (Type B):** Round 4

If Troll survived Act 1 (Besieged outcome): Troll activates this Act from his new position.
Ice Wights always active in Act 2.

**Victorious:** *"The runes speak. Something ancient has been moving through this pass — something that does not belong to Midgard. It is heading for Järnvik."*
→ One hero gains the Rune Knowledge token (used in Scenario 5 — see Special Rules there).

**Besieged:** *"You reach the shrine but the Ice Wights drive you back before you can read it fully. You catch only fragments."*
→ No Rune Knowledge token. Heroes carry the uncertainty into Scenario 5.

**[Act 2 → Act 3 Transition Strip]**
No suppression icons.

---

**Zone 5 — Act 3 Resolution**

**Win:** *"The pass is clear. The shrine has given you something — a name, a warning, a direction. Järnvik is behind you. Something is coming."*
- 3 Loot each + 20 XP.
- Legacy Card: won side.

**Loss:** *"The pass is behind you, barely. You did not learn what you needed. Järnvik will have to face what is coming blind."*
- 1 Loot each + 10 XP.
- Consolation: *"A mountain hermit sheltering in a cave near the shrine shares his fire and dried fish. Each hero removes 2 Exhaustion Tokens."*
- Legacy Card: lost side.

**Legacy Card — Scenario 4**

| Won | Lost |
|---|---|
| Negotiation actions roll with Advantage. | Troll attacks deal +1 damage in all scenarios. |

## Scenario 5 — The Night of All Shadows

*"They all came at once. As if something called them."*

---

### FRONT SIDE

**Zone 1 — Header**
- **Location:** Järnvik village, Midgard — the longest night
- **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Campaign Finale
- **Players:** 1-4
- **Monsters:** See Special Rules — varies by Legacy Card state

**Zone 2 — Hex Map**
```
Terrain: Full village map — largest scenario. Buildings create
obstacle corridors. Central square is open — 5×5 hex open area.
Four Hearth hexes at the square's corners. A Barricade hex at
each village entrance (north, east, west, south) — see Special Rules.
Shrine hex in village centre (same rune icon as Scenario 4).

Starting hexes:  Village centre, around shrine — 2-4 adjacent
Monster spawns:  Variable — see Special Rules
```

**Zone 3 — Act 1**
*"The darkness at the edge of Järnvik is moving. Every shadow is wrong tonight."*

**Monster setup — read all active Legacy Cards before placing:**
- **Draugr ×2** always spawn at the east barricade
- **Myling ×3** always spawn at the north barricade, staggered (one per round for 3 rounds)
- **Mara ×1** always spawns at the south barricade (face-down — hidden, as per her special rule)
- **Huldra ×1:** Spawns at west barricade ONLY if Scenario 1 Legacy Card is **lost side showing**. If won side showing, the Huldra does not appear — she is not their enemy tonight.

**Special Rule — Rune Knowledge:** If any hero carries the Rune Knowledge token from Scenario 4, they may spend it at the start of Act 1 to reveal the Mara's position immediately and grant all heroes Advantage on their first attack this scenario.

- **Primary trigger (Type C):** Act 1 ends after Round 3. Outcome depends on whether all four Barricade hexes remain unbroken (no monster has crossed through them).
- **Fallback:** Round 3 always ends Act 1.

**Victorious:** *"The barricades hold. Järnvik has bought itself time."*
→ Each hero removes 1 Exhaustion Token (the village rallies). Proceed to Act 2 with all Barricade hexes intact.

**Besieged:** *"One barricade falls. The screaming begins in the eastern quarter."*
→ One random Barricade hex is removed. One additional Draugr spawns at that entrance. Each hero gains 1 Exhaustion Token.

**Zone 6 — Special Rules**
- **Barricade hexes:** Monsters cannot cross a Barricade hex unless they move through it in combat. A hero may spend a Bottom Action to repair a broken barricade (restore it).
- **The Shrine:** Any hero who ends their turn on the shrine hex may remove 2 Exhaustion Tokens — the divine light of Scenario 4's runes still lingers.
- **Staggered Mylings:** Mylings spawn one per round at the start of each Monster Phase for the first 3 rounds. Not all at once.
- **The Huldra as Ally (won side only):** If the Huldra's Legacy Card is won side showing, she appears at the west barricade at the start of Act 2 as an ally — she activates after the heroes each round and attacks the nearest enemy to her, using her dice table. She cannot be commanded but will fight. She vanishes at Act 3 start regardless of outcome.

---

### BACK SIDE

**[Act 1 → Act 2 Transition Strip]**
No suppression icons — normal recovery and hand reset applies.

---

**Zone 4 — Act 2**
*"They are inside the village now. The only thing between them and the people of Järnvik is you."*

Act 2 is a full defense scenario — no fixed objective hex. Heroes must simply keep monsters away from the shrine and reduce the threat.

- **Primary trigger (Type A):** All monsters currently on the map are defeated
- **Secondary trigger:** Mara reduced to 2 HP — she flees regardless of other monsters
- **Fallback (Type B):** Round 6

**Reinforcements:** At the start of Round 3 of Act 2, roll D6. On 4-6, one additional Myling spawns at the most damaged barricade (or northern if all intact). The night is long.

**Victorious:** *"The last of them breaks. The village square is yours. The night is almost over."*
→ Proceed to Act 3 win resolution.

**Besieged:** *"There are too many. You fall back to the shrine — the last defensible ground."*
→ All monsters except the Mara are removed. The Mara remains for Act 3.

**[Act 2 → Act 3 Transition Strip]**
⚡ **Act 2→3 Exhaustion recovery SUPPRESSED** if Besieged outcome. The last stand allows no rest.
Hand reset applies normally regardless.

---

**Zone 5 — Act 3 Resolution**

**Win:** *"Dawn comes to Järnvik. The dead return to their mounds. The spirit retreats to her forest. The nightmare rides no more tonight. You stand in the village square — strangers no longer."*
- 4 Loot each + 20 XP.
- Bonus: The village of Järnvik owes the heroes a debt. Each hero gains 1 equipment item of their choice from the full loot shop at no cost.
- **World Tree Legacy Card instruction:** Place won side showing — *"Järnvik endures. Gain 3 Loot each entering next realm."*

**Win (Besieged Act 2, Mara defeated in Act 3):**
*"The last of them falls at dawn. Järnvik survives — bruised, grieving, but standing. You are the reason."*
- 3 Loot each + 20 XP.
- **World Tree Legacy Card instruction:** Place won side showing.

**Loss:** *"The Mara takes the last of the night. Järnvik does not fall — not entirely — but it is broken. When you leave, the village feels smaller than when you arrived."*
- 1 Loot each + 10 XP.
- Consolation: *"A child presses a carved wooden token into your hand as you leave. It is worth nothing. You keep it anyway. Gain 1 Fate Token each."*
- **World Tree Legacy Card instruction:** Place lost side showing — *"Järnvik falls. Begin next realm with 1 Exhaustion Token each."*

## Scenario Design Checklist

Every scenario sleeve must define before it is complete:

- [ ] Zone 1: Name, location, difficulty, player count, monster list
- [ ] Zone 2: Full hex map layout — starting positions, spawns, objectives, terrain
- [ ] Zone 3: Act 1 trigger type, round count, Victorious text, Besieged text
- [ ] Act 1→2 transition strip: suppression icons (if any)
- [ ] Zone 4: Act 2 trigger type, round count, Victorious text, Besieged text
- [ ] Act 2→3 transition strip: suppression icons (if any)
- [ ] Zone 5: Win text, loss text, consolation reward, Legacy Card instruction
- [ ] Zone 6: Special rules (if any)
- [ ] Legacy Card: Won side (≤15 words), Lost side (≤15 words)
- [ ] Branching objectives play mechanically differently (not just different resolution text)
- [ ] Fail-forward confirmed: no outcome is a dead end
