# Shadows of the Nine Realms — Level Progression

Heroes gain Experience (XP) across a campaign and grow in power through 9 levels. Progression is designed so a strong campaign (6 wins, 2 losses across 8 scenarios) reaches Level 9 naturally.

---

## XP Sources

XP comes from two sources, both always active regardless of session outcome.

### 1. Scenario XP
Awarded at Act 3 Resolution:

| Outcome | Scenario XP | Additional Reward |
|---|---|---|
| Win | 20 XP | Full loot as defined by Scenario Sleeve |
| Loss | 10 XP | 1 Fate Token each + named consolation reward (printed on Scenario Sleeve) |

Heroes gain experience even in defeat — every battle makes them wiser. The consolation reward is always specific and thematic, not a generic consolation prize.

### 2. Card XP
Certain ability card actions are marked with an **XP icon** (a runic symbol). These icons appear on actions that reflect what the class is *supposed to do* — playing into your class identity is what earns XP.

When you trigger an XP-marked action, place 1 XP Token on your character board immediately. At Act 3, total your tokens and add to your running XP.

**Expected card XP per session:** 8-12 XP (roughly 3-4 triggers across 7-10 rounds).

Each class has 3-4 XP-marked actions defined in Phase 2. See `04_classes.md`.

## XP Thresholds & Rewards

| Level | XP Needed | XP Gap | Rewards |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 | — | Starting board, 4 ability cards |
| 2 | 25 | 25 | Unlock 5th ability card |
| 3 | 55 | 30 | +2 Health |
| 4 | 90 | 35 | Signature ability enhanced |
| 5 | 120 | 30 | Dice threshold improvement (Strong Hit: 14-19) |
| 6 | 150 | 30 | Unlock 6th ability card |
| 7 | 180 | 30 | +2 Health |
| 8 | 210 | 30 | Signature ability mastery |
| 9 | 240 | 30 | **Saga Hero** — all dice thresholds improved (Strong Hit: 13-19) |

### Campaign Math
- **Strong campaign (6W / 2L):** 6×20 + 2×10 + 8 sessions × ~10 card XP = ~220 XP → **reaches Level 9**
- **Tough campaign (4W / 4L):** 4×20 + 4×10 + 8 sessions × ~10 card XP = ~200 XP → **reaches Level 8**

---

## Dice Threshold Progression

Rather than shifting the entire roll table, each threshold improvement widens the Strong Hit band by 1 — making the most rewarding outcome more accessible without trivialising the system.

| Level Range | Standard Hit | Strong Hit | Critical |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-4 (Base) | 7-14 | 15-19 | 20 |
| 5-8 (Tier 1) | 7-13 | **14-19** | 20 |
| 9 (Legendary) | 7-12 | **13-19** | 20 |

> The Character Board must indicate which threshold tier is currently active — the slider or dial design should reflect this. See Phase 7.

## Level 9 — Saga Hero

Reaching Level 9 mid-campaign unlocks **Saga Hero** status. The hero can no longer earn XP, but continues to grow through **Saga Points**.

### Saga Points
Any action that would normally trigger a card XP icon now generates a **Saga Point** instead (use XP tokens, different meaning). Saga Points are spent to directly benefit co-players:

| Cost | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 Saga Point | Give 1 Fate Token to any hero |
| 2 Saga Points | Any hero moves +2 hexes this turn |
| 3 Saga Points | Any hero negates damage from one attack |
| 4 Saga Points | Any hero immediately takes a bonus turn |

The Saga Hero fights *for* their companions rather than their own glory — a natural evolution of the cooperative philosophy and a powerful narrative moment in any campaign.

## Loot Shop

Loot is earned individually and spent between scenarios. See `09_campaign.md` for earning rates.

| Item | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Healing Draught | 2 Loot | Restore 3 Health (use once, during any session) |
| Weapon Upgrade | 4 Loot | +1 damage on all attacks (permanent) |
| Runic Charm | 5 Loot | Start next scenario with 2 Fate Tokens |
| New Ability | 6 Loot | Gain your 5th ability card early (before Level 2 XP unlock) |
| Blessing of the Gods | 8 Loot | Permanently remove 1 Exhaustion threshold — Strained triggers at 4 tokens instead of 3 |

> ⚠️ **Open design question:** Consider splitting Loot into gold (spendable currency) and items (physical equipment cards) for better narrative logic. See DEV_PLAN Open Design Questions.
