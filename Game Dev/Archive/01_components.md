# Shadows of the Nine Realms — Components & Setup

## The Book-Box Design

Shadows of the Nine Realms uses a revolutionary book-box design that opens like a tome, revealing the game world within. The compact A4 format (approximately 100-150mm thickness) makes it perfect for travel.

### Left Half — The Battlefield & Storage
Contains the dry-erase hex grid map, card storage compartments with dividers for each class, token wells, and the scenario sleeve slot. The scenario sleeve slides over this half during play, adding terrain details without requiring separate tiles.

### Right Half — Player Dashboards
Four player dashboards with health/exhaustion tracking, ability card slots, dice result reference areas, and condition markers. Each player claims one dashboard for their hero.

### The Spine — Storage Vault
The center spine contains organized compartments for dice, miniatures, and the campaign journal. Everything has its place.

## Setup

### 1. Prepare the Book-Box
Open the book-box and place it in the center of your play area. Slide the appropriate Scenario Sleeve over the left half, aligning the terrain markings with the hex grid beneath.

### 2. Choose Your Heroes
Each player selects a character class and takes the corresponding Character Board. Place your hero miniature on one of the starting hexes marked on the scenario sleeve. Take your ability cards and arrange them on your player dashboard.

> **First game recommendation:** Einherjar (tank), Valkyrie (damage), and Seidr (support).

### 3. Place Monsters
Place monster tokens on the hexes indicated by the scenario sleeve. Each monster type has a corresponding card showing its stats and AI behavior. Set these cards near the map for reference.

### 4. Prepare Tokens
Create a supply of Fate Tokens, Exhaustion Tokens, and Loot Tokens within reach of all players. Each player marks their starting Health on their dashboard (varies by class).

### 5. Campaign Setup
If playing a campaign, record your party's current level, available loot, and any persistent story choices in the Campaign Journal. New campaigns start at Level 1.
