# Shadows of the Nine Realms — Components & Setup

## The Book-Box Design

Shadows of the Nine Realms uses a book-box design that opens like a tome, revealing the game world within. The compact A4 format (approximately 100-150mm thickness) makes it perfect for travel. Setup takes under 5 minutes.

### Left Half — The Battlefield & Storage
Contains the dry-erase hex grid map, card storage compartments with dividers for each class, token wells, and the scenario sleeve slot. The scenario sleeve slides over this half during play, adding terrain details without requiring separate tiles.

### Right Half — Player Dashboards
Four player dashboards with health/exhaustion tracking, ability card slots, dice result reference areas, and condition markers. Each player claims one dashboard for their hero.

### The Spine — Storage Vault
The center spine contains organised compartments for dice, miniatures, and **6 Legacy Card slots** (numbered 1-6, won/lost face visible). Legacy Cards accumulate here across a campaign, replacing the need for a campaign journal. Everything has its place.

## The Character Board

Each hero's Character Board is a physical, resettable progression board containing:

- **Sliding XP Track** — notched slider along the board edge, clicks into positions 1-9 (level). Slide back to 1 to start a new campaign.
- **Ability Slots with Token Pegs** — 6 printed ability slots. At Level 1, slots 5 and 6 are blocked by cover pegs. Remove covers as abilities are unlocked. Resettable by replacing covers.
- **Health/Exhaustion Dial** — rotating paper dial behind a window on the board. Spin to track damage and exhaustion, spin back to reset.
- **Exhaustion Token Slots** — 6 visible slots. Threshold markers printed at slots 3 (Strained) and 5 (Exhausted) — no rulebook lookup needed mid-game.
- **Loot Token Slots** — 10 slots for individual loot tokens.

**Session start takes under 30 seconds per player:** slide XP track to current level, remove unlocked ability covers, set health dial.

## Setup

### 1. Prepare the Book-Box
Open the book-box and place it in the center of your play area. Slide the appropriate Scenario Sleeve over the left half, aligning the terrain markings with the hex grid beneath.

### 2. Read the Scenario Sleeve
Read the front of the sleeve — Scenario Header (Zone 1), Map layout (Zone 2), Act 1 rules (Zone 3), and any Special Rules (Zone 6). Flip to the back and read Act 2 (Zone 4) and Act 3 (Zone 5). Note the transition strips between Acts.

### 3. Read Active Legacy Cards
Scan the spine slots left to right. Note any Legacy Card effects relevant to this scenario's location or type. Most sessions will have 2-3 relevant cards. Later slots override earlier slots on conflicts.

### 4. Choose Your Heroes
Each player selects a character class and takes the corresponding Character Board. Set the XP slider to your current level and remove ability covers for any unlocked slots. Place your hero miniature on a starting hex marked on the scenario sleeve.

> **First game recommendation:** Einherjar (tank), Valkyrie (damage), and Seidr (support).

### 5. Place Monsters
Place monster tokens on the hexes indicated by the scenario sleeve. Set the corresponding Monster Cards nearby for reference.

### 6. Prepare Tokens
Distribute Fate Tokens, Exhaustion Tokens, Loot Tokens, and XP Tokens within reach of all players. Each player sets their starting Health on their dial (varies by class).
