# Shadows of the Nine Realms — Components & Setup

## The Book-Box Design

Shadows of the Nine Realms uses a book-box design that opens like a tome, revealing the game world within. The compact A4 format (approximately 100-150mm thickness) makes it perfect for travel. Setup takes under 5 minutes.

### Left Half — The Battlefield & Storage
Contains the dry-erase hex grid map, the Scenario Sleeve slot, token wells, and the D20 die. The Scenario Sleeve slides over this half during play, adding terrain and rules without requiring separate tiles.

### Right Half — Character Sleeve Storage
Houses **4 Character Sleeve slots** — one per active hero. Each Character Sleeve is a slim physical pocket containing everything belonging to that hero: Character Board, full ability card pool, equipment items, class-specific tokens, Loot Tokens, and XP Tokens. The box holds 4 sleeves at a time. When changing active characters, simply swap sleeves.

> **Modular loading:** There are 6 character classes total, but the box holds 4 at a time. Before a campaign, choose your 4 active characters and load their sleeves. Portability comes from this constraint — the box is always campaign-ready, never overstuffed.

### The Spine — Storage Vault
The centre spine contains compartments for dice, miniatures, shared tokens (Fate, Exhaustion), and **two tiers of Legacy Card slots:**
- **6 Realm Legacy Card slots** — for the current realm campaign
- **6 World Tree Legacy Card slots** — permanent across the grand campaign

Legacy Cards accumulate here, replacing the need for a campaign journal.

> ⚠️ **Physical design note:** A three-panel unfold is under consideration — a third panel folding out from the right half to house the Legacy Card slots and grand campaign map, keeping the battlefield clean during play. See Phase 7.

## The Character Board

Each hero's Character Board is a physical, resettable progression board stored in their Character Sleeve. It contains:

- **Sliding XP Track** — notched slider along the board edge, clicks into positions 1-9 (level). Slide back to 1 to start a new campaign.
- **Ability Slots with Token Pegs** — slots matching the class's maximum pool size. At Level 1, unlocked slots are open; locked slots have cover pegs. Remove covers as abilities are unlocked. Resettable by replacing covers.
- **Health/Exhaustion Dial** — rotating paper dial behind a window on the board. Spin to track damage and exhaustion, spin back to reset.
- **Exhaustion Token Slots** — 6 visible slots. Threshold markers printed at slots 3 (Strained) and 5 (Exhausted) — no rulebook lookup needed mid-game.
- **Loot Token Slots** — 10 slots for individual loot tokens.
- **Equipment Slots** — 3 dedicated slots (Weapon / Armour / Relic). Equipment Cards slide into or sit on these slots. Visible at a glance during play.

**Session start takes under 30 seconds per player:** slide XP track to current level, remove unlocked ability covers, set health dial, check equipped items.

## The Character Sleeve

Each Character Sleeve is a self-contained hero kit. It stores:

| Contents | Notes |
|---|---|
| Character Board | Physical board with all dials and sliders |
| Ability Card Pool | All unlocked cards for the current level (max 6 at Level 6+) |
| Equipment Cards | Up to 3 equipped items (Weapon / Armour / Relic) |
| Unequipped Items | Any purchased but unequipped equipment |
| Class-Specific Tokens | e.g. Curse Tokens (Nidstang) |
| Loot Tokens | Individual stash (max 10) |
| XP Tokens | Individual XP count |

When swapping characters between campaigns, simply swap the sleeve. Everything is self-contained.

## Card Selection — Before Each Act

At each Act transition where the hand reset is **not suppressed**, players select which cards from their full pool to bring into the upcoming Act. Hand size varies by class and player count — see `04_classes.md` for class-specific hand sizes.

**Selection timing:**
- **Before Act 1:** Read the Scenario Sleeve front (Zones 1, 2, 3, 6), then select
- **Act 1 → Act 2 (if not suppressed):** Read the transition strip and Zone 4, then re-select
- **Act 2 → Act 3 (if not suppressed):** Read the transition strip and Zone 5, then re-select
- **Suppressed transition:** No re-selection. Carry forward whatever remains in hand.

Cards not selected stay in the sleeve — they do not exist mechanically during that Act.

## Setup

### 1. Prepare the Book-Box
Open the book-box and place it in the centre of your play area. Slide the appropriate Scenario Sleeve over the left half, aligning terrain markings with the hex grid.

### 2. Read the Scenario Sleeve
Read the front — Zones 1, 2, 3, and 6. Flip to the back and read the Act 1→2 transition strip, Zone 4, Act 2→3 transition strip, and Zone 5. Note any suppression icons.

### 3. Read Active Legacy Cards
Scan Realm Legacy Card slots left to right, then World Tree Legacy Card slots. Note relevant effects. Later slots override earlier on conflicts. Most sessions: 2-3 relevant cards total.

### 4. Load Character Sleeves
Each player takes their Character Sleeve, sets the XP slider to current level, removes unlocked ability covers, sets the health dial, and checks equipped items.

> **First game recommendation:** Einherjar (tank), Valkyrie (damage), and Seidr (support).

### 5. Select Opening Hand
Each player selects their Act 1 hand from their full card pool. Read the Scenario Sleeve front carefully — Act 1's trigger type, terrain, and special rules should inform selection.

### 6. Place Monsters
Place monster tokens on spawn hexes indicated by the Scenario Sleeve. Set Monster Cards nearby for reference.

### 7. Prepare Shared Tokens
Place Fate Tokens and Exhaustion Tokens in a shared pool within reach of all players.
