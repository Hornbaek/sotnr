# Shadows of the Nine Realms — Combat System

## The D20 System

When you perform an attack, roll the D20 and consult your Character Board's Dice Result Table. Each class has unique thresholds and effects based on their specialty.

The game uses a **single D20**, rolled twice when Advantage or Disadvantage applies. Players may always add more D20s for convenience.

---

## Fail Forward Mechanics

Shadows of the Nine Realms uses a fail-forward philosophy at every level. Even poor rolls accomplish something — the action never stalls.

| Roll | Result | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1-3 | Glancing Blow | Deal 1 damage OR minor bonus effect |
| 4-6 | Weak Hit | Half damage (round down), no bonus effects |
| 7-14 | Standard Hit | Full damage/effect, normal bonuses |
| 15-19 | Strong Hit | Full damage + bonus effect OR Fate Token |
| 20 | Critical Hit | Double damage + all bonuses + 2 Fate Tokens |

### Dice Threshold Progression

As heroes level up, the Strong Hit band widens — making the most rewarding outcomes more accessible without trivialising the system:

| Level | Glancing | Weak | Standard | Strong | Critical |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1-4 (Base) | 1-3 | 4-6 | 7-14 | 15-19 | 20 |
| 5-8 (Tier 1) | 1-3 | 4-6 | 7-13 | **14-19** | 20 |
| 9 (Legendary) | 1-3 | 4-6 | 7-12 | **13-19** | 20 |

## Advantage & Disadvantage

Some effects grant Advantage or Disadvantage on rolls.

**Mechanic:** Roll the D20 **twice** (or roll one D20 twice in sequence).
- **Advantage:** Keep the **higher** result
- **Disadvantage:** Keep the **lower** result

If you have both Advantage and Disadvantage simultaneously, they cancel out — roll once as normal. Multiple sources of the same type never stack.

### Sources

**Advantage:**
| Source | Trigger |
|---|---|
| Flanking | Attacking from behind the target |
| Higher Ground | Attacking from an elevated hex |
| Strengthen condition | Active on the attacker |
| Legacy Card | Specific won-side bonuses |

**Disadvantage:**
| Source | Trigger |
|---|---|
| Strained state | 3-4 Exhaustion Tokens on attacker |
| Partial Obscurement | Line of sight partially blocked |
| Muddle condition | Active on the attacker |
| Legacy Card | Specific lost-side penalties |

---

## Fate Tokens

Fate Tokens represent the Norns' favour and can be spent to influence destiny.

**Maximum: 3 Fate Tokens per hero at any time.**

### Earning Fate Tokens
- Strong Hit (15-19): gain 1 Fate Token OR take bonus effect
- Critical Hit (20): gain 2 Fate Tokens automatically
- Certain class ability card actions (XP-marked cards)

### Spending Fate Tokens

| Cost | Effect | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Fate Token | Reroll the D20 (must use new result) | Before consulting Dice Table |
| 1 Fate Token | Add +2 to a D20 result | After rolling, before consulting Dice Table |
| 1 Fate Token | Negate 1 Exhaustion you would suffer | When Exhaustion is applied |
| 1 Fate Token | Move 1 additional hex | As part of any movement action |

> **Saga Hero spend:** Heroes at Level 9 spend Fate Tokens as Saga Points to benefit co-players. See `05_progression.md`.

## Conditions

Conditions are applied by specific monster abilities, class actions, and scenario effects.

| Condition | Effect |
|---|---|
| Wound | Suffer 1 damage at start of each turn |
| Poison | Suffer 1 damage; healing reduced by 1 |
| Immobilize | Cannot perform Move actions |
| Disarm | Cannot perform Attack actions |
| Stun | Skip your entire turn |
| Muddle | All attacks roll at Disadvantage |
| Strengthen | All attacks roll at Advantage |
| Invisible | Cannot be targeted by monsters |
| Shield X | Reduce next X damage by X |
